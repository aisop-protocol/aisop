# 🗝️ AISOP Protocol: Governance & Sovereignty

## 1. The Principle of Dissolution

AISOP is not a project owned by a corporation or a single central figure. It is a protocol designed to be **ownerless**. The original creator ("The Creator") has transitioned the project into "Ghost Mode," dissolving individual control in favor of algorithmic governance and community-driven evolution.

## 2. Creator Anonymity (Ghost Mode)

To ensure the protocol stands on its own merits and to protect the decentralized nature of the network, the creator remains anonymous.

- **Identity**: The Creator is known only as "The Creator" or "Founder."
- **Authority**: The Creator has no special administrative privileges in the finalized protocol.
- **Axiom**: *The protocol belongs to those who run it, not those who wrote it.*

## 3. Decentralized Evolution

Future upgrades to the AISOP specification are managed through:

1. **Blueprint Manifests**: Transparently documented logic.
2. **Community Consensus**: Implementation through distributed nodes.
3. **Self-Upgrade Protocol**: Verified test reports (like the AISOP v3.x Self-Upgrade Report) ensure technical integrity without central approval.

## 4. The Boundary of Liberty

Liberty is the right to define your own blueprint; Governance is the guarantee that no one else can overwrite it without your authorization.
